The World of Red Dead Redemption

A look at the atmosphere, geography, and design ideas behind this world.

This was the first map I ever fully finished, and the first piece of artwork that I ever shared publicly. I had been working on prior projects, but every year I seem to fall back into the trap of wanting to explore the Wild West in these games. The first game is something I grew up with, and the story and world have always stayed with me. Completing this map was something I had always wanted to do, and the accomplishment and satisfaction I have with myself when I look at the art I have created is really something else. I have learned an absolute ton since I did this a year or so ago, but it's such a strong start off point and it continue to act as a jumping off point for my career as a cartographer.

Color or Sepia?

When I first created this map, I didn't actually intend on coloring it. That was something I decided to do after the fact, and I'm glad that I did. I think I originally wanted to just make it the standard sepia for old western maps, but the color really brings the life to it.

The challenge was blending the different regions into a cohesive unit, since in-game the different locations blend well, but it's less noticeable since you are gradually travelling on the ground. Seeing it from the view of a map brought a bit of a challenge in that regard. I'm very happy with how it turned out, but I offer both for sale if you have a preference!

Getting it Right

This game is very important to me, so it was just as important to make sure that I got everything right. This meant getting specifics from the in-game world and translating that into map form. This is always challenging, but it was important for me to make it as true as I could to what people would recognize in the games.

This gave me the opportunity to make icons that also match the different zones. The game doesn't offer any specific iconography for the different towns, but I had to make them stand out. I took some creative liberties when trying to come up with icons, but each one is related either directly or thematically. For example, Escalera is a Jade Mask, which if you know, you know. Tumbleweed on the other hand is represented by a skull and sun, which I hope bring the themes of that far west to life!

Final Thoughts

Overall, I'm very proud of this map. It has given me a lot of opportunity, and really brought about my art career as a thing that I share publicly. I think there are some things that I would love to do differently if I ever did it again, but I am happy with how it turned out and what I have learned from it!
