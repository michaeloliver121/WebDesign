USACE Albeni Falls Dam Project

My first real world commission!

This was a commission I did for the US Army Corp of Engineers. It's a map showcasing over 4000+ acres of public land that they oversee, highlighting the main recreational sites that they oversee. This job was my first ever commission, and it was definitely difficult to do since I mainly focus on fictional worlds.

If I'm being honest, I didn't enjoy this as much as I would have liked. I appreciate the commission, and the concept of creating something for someone else, but I find myself less enthusiastic to draw places in the real world. There is something so fascinating about fictional worlds that allow me to get sucked into them and learn more about them. It's the same feeling as reading a fantasy series vs some nonfiction biography. While there is some interesting stuff there, your imagination isn't in the same place.

Visual Direction & Design

This style was a bit difficult to visualize for me, since it couldn't be as detailed as I would have liked it to be. This map is intended for print on smaller letter sheets, so the design needed to be mainly informational AND legible. I wanted to add some creativity though, and I think I was able to bridge that gap of being too detailed while also giving it some life. Some of the prior work that USACE was using for this was standard governmental designs, which are boring to look at. This isn't boring to look at, and it also gets the important info across.

Final Thoughts

I'm happy I was able to complete this, and I hope that it leads to more opportunities. Like all of my art, I learn so much from it that I feel more confident to take on whatever comes next!
